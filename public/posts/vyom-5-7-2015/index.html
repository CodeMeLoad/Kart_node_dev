The A-team
5 Jul 2015
Vyom Srivastava, senior CPR member writes on his experiences with FS and the TeamKART CPR team.

“I will burn all the bridges behind me and stake my entire future on my ability to get what I want”
-Edwin Barnes

Just before the splendid performance in Formula Design Challenge 2015, TeamKART stood on a similar verge on which Barnes once stood. The project was facing a high tide and we all were drifting by its force. The only life boat that we had, was an opportunity to participate in FDC '15.

The competition was closing day by day. November was the month when the whole team was struggling to meet the deadlines and to re-invent an already made car to meet new competition’s parameters. The competition was scheduled in January and the work seemed to be endless. Thousands of man hours and 5 straight sleepless nights finally paid off when we got 2nd position in Cost Report analysis and Business presentation Competition. The project, once again raised the flag of IIT Kharagpur just like it happened in FSUK Silverstone '13!

On that day, we learned one thing.

First build an A-class team, then an A-class car

Quite a common line in formula world, but indeed a line to cherish. I might not be the best person to advise you on how to make cars but after spending two years in a young Formula Student team, I have learned that the integrity of a team is the most important ingredient of a growing FS team.

Corporate and public relations team of TeamKART is the second pillar of the team. I am usually asked, what exactly do you guys do in a ‘car making project’? Right, I am tired of answering this question and I am not going to answer it again. But the thing to notice is that the work of a CPR team of any Formula Student team is not very discernible on the ultimate product, which is a formula prototype car.

I don’t know how much people acknowledge CPR’s work, but inside the team, the spirits of appreciation and respect for each other’s work is impeccable.

And that’s what makes us an A-class team!

I feel privileged that I got a chance to work in such a prodigious team.
